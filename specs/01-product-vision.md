# Spec 01: Product Vision

## Purpose

Define what game we are building and what player fantasy we need to deliver.

## Authority

This spec is authoritative for product intent, player fantasy, and high-level
success direction.

## Dependencies

- Spec 02 for the gameplay loop translation of this vision.
- Spec 07 for delivery constraints and scope discipline.

## High Concept

- Working title: Blackwake Isles
- Genre: arcade naval action with exploration and light progression
- Platform: web browser
- Stack target: Three.js with JavaScript or TypeScript
- Delivery strategy: singleplayer first, multiplayer-ready later

## Product Goals

The first playable prototype must prove:
- Naval movement feels satisfying.
- Combat is readable and replayable.
- The codebase can support multiplayer later without a full rewrite.

## Design Pillars

1. Immediate fun from sailing and cannon combat.
2. Readable systems with simple resources and upgrades.
3. Strong pirate fantasy through islands, ports, storms, and loot.
4. Expandable architecture that keeps future networking viable.

## Player Fantasy

The player should feel like:
- Captain of a growing pirate ship.
- Explorer of dangerous waters.
- Hunter of merchants and rival pirates.
- Treasure seeker balancing greed and risk.

## One Sentence Pitch

A stylized browser pirate game where you sail a ship, fight enemies, collect
loot, and upgrade your vessel in a compact open sea designed for future
multiplayer expansion.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The project pitch, goals, and design pillars are consistent across README and
  spec documents.
- No feature proposal conflicts with the stated product fantasy.
- MVP planning docs map back to at least one product goal in this spec.
